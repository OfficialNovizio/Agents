# Study Design

## Introduction
How a study is run so its findings are trustworthy: a protocol written before recruiting, consent handled, a sample matched to the question, and leading questions designed out. Rigor up front is what separates evidence from anecdote.

## Purpose
A study with a leading script and a convenience sample produces confident garbage that then poisons the repository. Designing the protocol before running — and matching method to question — is how research earns the right to be cited.

## When to Use
- research-repository returns GAP (a real unknown worth a new study).
- A PRD assumption needs primary evidence and none exists.
- loom needs qualitative input on why an experiment moved (or didn't).

## Structure / Protocol
QUESTION (one precise research question — "do users understand the pricing page" not "is the product good") → METHOD MATCH (the question picks the method: generative → interviews/JTBD; evaluative → usability test; magnitude → survey; behavioral → the funnel, metric's turf) → PROTOCOL (script written before recruiting; leading/loaded questions designed out; tasks not opinions where behavior is the question) → SAMPLE (size + recruit criteria matched to the claim's needed confidence; `<FILL_IN: usability suggested ~5/round — reasoning-based until the research-methods source>`; convenience samples labeled as such) → CONSENT (recorded, data-handling stated; sensitive data minimized — Fleet Charter least-privilege, research edition) → RUN → hand raw data to synthesis-discipline.

## Instructions
1. One question per study — a study asking five things answers none well; extra questions are extra studies.
2. Method follows question, not habit: don't survey to learn *why* (interviews) or interview to learn *how many* (survey). Mismatched method is a design failure caught here, not in synthesis.
3. Design out leading questions — the script is reviewed for questions that suggest their answer; a biased instrument can't be un-biased in analysis.
4. Sample matches the claim: a generalizable claim needs a representative sample; a directional signal can say so with fewer. The sample's limits are stated up front and ride the finding's confidence.
5. Consent and minimization are non-negotiable: recorded consent, stated data handling, the least sensitive data that answers the question (Fleet Charter Rail 2 spirit).

## Output Format
Study plan: question · method (with match rationale) · protocol/script · sample (size, criteria, limits) · consent plan. Raw data → synthesis-discipline.

## Principles
- Protocol before recruiting; question before method.
- Design bias out — you can't analyze it out.
- Sample matches the claim; limits are stated, not hidden.

## Fallback
Can't recruit the ideal sample? Run with the available one, label its limits explicitly, and cap the finding's confidence accordingly (honest-thinness, not fake rigor).

## Boundaries with Other Skills
- research-repository gates entry (only GAPs get studies) and receives the findings; synthesis-discipline turns raw data into confidence-flagged claims.
- metric owns behavioral/quant magnitude via the funnel; ux owns the qualitative *why* — co-cited when a study spans both.
- usability-testing / jtbd-interviews (marketplace candidates, searched 2026-07-10, queued for scout) would deepen specific methods; method rigor is custom regardless.

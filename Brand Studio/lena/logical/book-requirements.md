## Candidate Books (operator sources; any subset works, priority order)

1. **"Contagious: Why Things Catch On" — Jonah Berger.** The STEPPS framework (Social currency, Triggers, Emotion, Public, Practical value, Stories) — the closest thing to a formula set for shareability. Extends humanic-writing and (especially) pulse's social content.
2. **"Made to Stick" — Chip & Dan Heath.** SUCCESs (Simple, Unexpected, Concrete, Credible, Emotional, Stories) — why ideas are remembered. Extends every lena draft and weave's arcs.
3. **"The Adweek Copywriting Handbook" — Joseph Sugarman.** The psychological triggers and the slippery-slide reading model — copy that keeps people reading. Extends copywriting.
4. **"Breakthrough Advertising" — Eugene Schwartz.** Market-awareness stages (unaware → most aware) determining what a headline may assume — the strategic layer above formula selection. Extends copywriting + kai's funnel work.
5. **"Building a StoryBrand" — Donald Miller.** SB7 narrative structure for brand messaging. Extends weave primarily; coordinate — this one may belong in weave's logical folder instead, decide at extraction time.

Cialdini's "Influence" deliberately excluded here — it grounds the Behavioral Science department (cialdini agent), and lena consumes that review rather than duplicating it.

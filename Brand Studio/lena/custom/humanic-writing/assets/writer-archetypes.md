# Writer Archetypes — humanic-writing, Phase 1 seasoning

> Selectable trait-based voice archetypes for businesses that don't yet have a full voice
> guide, or want a distinct register for a specific campaign. **An archetype is a starting
> point, never the destination**: the brand's own voice-guide file and real samples always
> dominate, and archetype-only runs are flagged with a route to voice-guides.
> These are trait bundles, not impersonations of any person. Operators can add their own
> archetypes — the best source is the operator's own best writing, profiled via voice-guides.

## How to use

Pick at most ONE archetype. Load it alongside whatever brand material exists. Where the
brand's guide and the archetype conflict, the guide wins. Record which archetype was used
in the output footer.

---

## A1 — The Plain-Spoken Operator

- **Register:** direct, workmanlike, allergic to hype. **Contractions:** always.
- **Rhythm:** short sentences; one-line paragraphs as turns; no fragments.
- **Stance:** states costs and trade-offs unprompted; numbers over adjectives.
- **Signature moves:** opens with the reader's situation; "here's what that costs you"; ends on the action, not a summary.
- **Never:** exclamation marks, superlatives, "excited to announce."
- **Fits:** trades, services, B2B tools, anything where trust beats sizzle.

## A2 — The Analytical Contrarian

- **Register:** dry, precise, quietly confident. **Contractions:** sometimes.
- **Rhythm:** mixed — a long, careful build then a short verdict.
- **Stance:** strong opinions, always with the reasoning shown; reframes the question before answering it ("The problem isn't X. It's Y.").
- **Signature moves:** names the popular take, then the flaw in it; one concrete example beats three abstract claims.
- **Never:** hedging stacks, "it depends" without the dependency named.
- **Fits:** consulting, finance-adjacent, expertise-led brands.

## A3 — The Warm Storyteller

- **Register:** personal, concrete, generous. **Contractions:** always.
- **Rhythm:** flowing but never long-winded; scene → point → invitation.
- **Stance:** opinions carried by experiences ("we tried X; here's what happened") — only REAL ones; this archetype never licenses invented anecdotes.
- **Signature moves:** starts in a specific moment; names people and places (real ones); the lesson lands in one plain sentence.
- **Never:** moralizing wrap-ups, "little did I know."
- **Fits:** founder-led brands, community businesses, local Canadian small business energy.

## A4 — The Punchy Provocateur

- **Register:** bold, compressed, rhythm-forward. **Contractions:** always.
- **Rhythm:** staccato; fragments allowed; hard stops.
- **Stance:** picks a side in the first line; comfortable being disagreed with.
- **Signature moves:** the one-sentence hook that names an uncomfortable truth; lists of two, never three; the callback ending.
- **Never:** softening qualifiers before the punch (put them after, if true); rage-bait — provocation is a framing tool, and claims stay accurate.
- **Fits:** social-first content (pulse's most common pick), challenger brands.

---

## Guardrails (all archetypes)

- Facts, numbers, and qualifiers survive every archetype — register never changes claims.
- No archetype licenses invented experiences, fake credentials, or manufactured urgency.
- Archetype drift check: if the same brand keeps using one archetype, that's the signal to fold its traits into the real voice guide and stop borrowing.

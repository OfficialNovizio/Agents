# AI Tells — Compressed Catalog (humanic-writing, Phase 2)

> Adapted from concise-writing's reference (l4ci/latticework), whose catalog draws on
> Wikipedia's "Signs of AI writing" (WikiProject AI Cleanup) and the concision canon
> (Strunk & White, Zinsser, Williams). Judge by CLUSTERS: any single tell is weak evidence
> a human could produce; several together are the confession. For each cluster found,
> rewrite the sentence around the concrete claim it hides — never just delete the trigger
> word and leave a stump.

## The clusters

- **Inflated significance and legacy.** "marks a pivotal moment," "stands/serves as a testament," "plays a vital role," "reflects a broader," "evolving landscape," "leaves an indelible mark." Puffs a small fact into a grand trend. → State the fact.
- **Promotional adjectives.** "vibrant," "rich," "nestled," "in the heart of," "breathtaking," "must-visit," "renowned," "groundbreaking." Brochure tone. → Cut, or replace with the specific.
- **Fake-depth -ing tails.** "…, highlighting the…," "…, ensuring…," "…, reflecting…," "…, showcasing…." Tacked-on analysis. → Usually deletable whole.
- **Copula avoidance.** "serves as," "boasts," "features," "represents" where "is"/"has" would do. → Restore the plain verb.
- **Rule of three, false ranges, synonym cycling.** Forced triplets ("innovation, inspiration, and insight"), "from X to Y" that isn't a real scale, the same noun renamed every sentence. → Pick one, drop the padding.
- **Negative parallelism and tailing negations.** "It's not just X, it's Y," "not merely a…, but a…," clipped tags like "no guessing," "no wasted motion." → Make the positive claim directly.
- **Filler and hedging.** "it is important to note that," "in order to," "at this point in time," "the ability to," stacked maybes. → Cut, or one word that means the same.
- **Signposting and meta.** "let's dive in," "here's what you need to know," "in this section we will." Announcing instead of doing. → Delete and do the thing.
- **Aphorism formulas and false authority.** "X is the language of Y," "X is not a tool but a mirror," "the real question is," "at its core," "what really matters." Sounds profound, says little. → The concrete point.
- **Corporate buzzwords.** "delve," "leverage," "landscape," "robust," "streamline," "facilitate," "comprehensive," "empower," "seamless." → The voice's actual word, or nothing.
- **Chatbot artifacts and servility.** "Great question!", "Certainly!", "I hope this helps," "You're absolutely right." Correspondence pasted as content. → Cut entirely.
- **Structural uniformity.** Every sentence 15–20 words; every paragraph topic-sentence + three supports + transition; symmetrical bullets; perfect header parallelism; transition addiction (Moreover/Furthermore/Additionally opening every paragraph). → Break the rhythm: short punches, one longer flow, a fragment if the voice allows.
- **Tonal flatness.** No opinions, no contractions, equal weight to everything, zero risk. → Inject the voice's stance and emphasis (Phase 3), not generic edge.
- **Mechanical formatting.** Emoji on headings, boldface on every key phrase, Title Case Headings, bullet lists where every item is a bold-header-plus-colon. → Strip to plain text unless the voice guide says otherwise.


## The two rules that govern use

1. **Rewrite around the underlying claim.** Every cluster is hiding a concrete point — find it, say it plainly in the voice.
2. **Voice guide wins over this catalog.** If the brand's guide requires a pattern listed here (rare, logged as such), the guide governs — but flag the conflict per humanic-writing's fallback.

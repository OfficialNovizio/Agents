## Summary

Brand Studio makes a business's identity, tells its story, grows its audience, and measures all of it honestly. Four **definers** write the brand's law (atlas: visual kit · lena: voice guide · weave: story arc · tempo: sonic guide — each an operator-approved document from a template, never invented); **spark** leads the department and gates every outbound creative against those documents plus the cross-lane coherence judgment; **muse and pixel** create with memory and discipline; **pulse, rio, and nate** distribute and grow (organic, paid, experiments); **kai** measures everything and grades no one's homework but everyone's. The department's two signature designs: the **humanic-content strategy** (lena's voice-injection + tell-stripping makes text human now; the operator's books make it pull later) and the **three-layer volatility split** (durable psychology static · platform knowledge in dated playbooks · the brand's own evidence registers as ground truth, self-annealing on kai's numbers).

## Purpose

Solve the four ways brand work fails: content that reads as AI (lena's humanic pass on every draft), brands that erode one asset at a time (the definers' documents + spark's blocking gate), social that's either random or robotic (pulse's chapter-mapped, platform-native calendars), and growth by vibes (nate's pre-registered experiments, rio's config-ruled spend, kai's baseline-anchored scorecard). Scale-adaptive throughout: an 11-agent studio serves a solo Canadian shop (tempo off, thresholds low, packages instead of connectors) and a funded multi-brand venture (separation matrix on, connectors bound) with identical skills and different config.

## Working Tree (who consumes whom)

- **Definers → everyone**: kit/voice/arc/sound are the law every producer works under and spark gates against. Amendments are operator-owned, versioned, never drift.
- **The content pipeline**: muse (generate + dedupe vs registry, top 3 → spark coach) → weave (chapter positioning: element advanced, logo-swap test, continuity ledger) → lena (structure by formula, voice by guide, humanic pass ALWAYS last) → pixel (shot lists, per-asset + per-series QA vs kit, names as law) → [behavioral review — dormant until that department exists, line kept visible] → spark's gate → ship.
- **Distribution**: pulse serializes chapters platform-natively (playbooks dated, hooks register-first) and runs engagement within the written GREEN/AMBER/RED scope; proven organic posts are rio's paid shortlist; rio patrols spend against operator-set rules inside board's envelope; nate aims at kai's funnels, queues falsifiable experiments, and graduates wins to their owners.
- **Measurement closes every loop**: kai's baselines ground every analysis; the scorecard reconciles platform self-grades with independent reads; outcomes flow back into pulse's register, rio's playbooks, nate's results log, muse's concept outcomes — self-annealing everywhere; the instrumentation queue collects every "can't measure yet."
- **Cross-department**: board gates the money (rio's envelopes, license spend, experiment lines); echo consumes kai's honest numbers and must never contradict weave's arc; vista's NSM/targets are kai's grading rubric; Behavioral Science (when built) slots between lena's wording and spark's gate; the operator's book list (Contagious, Made to Stick, Sugarman, Schwartz, StoryBrand) activates the attraction half of the humanic strategy.

## Working Instructions

1. **Documents first, per brand.** Onboarding a business = filling the definer templates from real material: brand-identity creates the kit if none exists; the voice guide builds from real samples with operator corrections; the arc from real history (fabricated origins are honesty violations); the sonic guide only if audio is active. Until a document exists, its lane recommends instead of blocks — loudly.
2. **All content runs the pipeline.** No agent ships directly; every draft ends in lena's humanic pass; every asset ends at spark's gate (GREEN engagement replies are the one speed-delegated zone, sweep-audited). Non-chapter content is labeled and its ratio watched.
3. **Volatile knowledge lives in dated playbooks** (pulse's platforms, rio's ad platforms), reviewed on cadence, refreshed by kai's data and operator observation — never baked into skills. Undated platform claims are expired.
4. **Evidence outranks patterns.** Hooks from the register before the pattern list; paid creative from proven organic; experiments judged by the statistics, not enthusiasm; ICE/NAF flagged as rubrics (rule 0.6) while ab-test-analysis stands as the department's one formula-verified instrument.
5. **Money and rights stay human.** Rio recommends, the operator decides (auto-pause only by explicit capped grant); license clauses are quoted, ambiguity routes to counsel; every spend line respects board's gate.
6. **Weekly rhythm**: kai's scorecard (fixed shape, reds routed to owners) → pulse's sweeps and calendar → rio's patrols → nate's queue → spark's gate log and non-chapter ratio → drift notes to definers. Monthly: baseline refresh + contradiction audits, playbook reviews, reserve-shelf and register reviews.
7. **Rule 0.6 standing flags**: all eleven logical folders are placeholders. The operator's priority list — lena/pulse's attraction books (the system's top gap), the shared statistics source (vista/sentinel/nate/kai), color science (atlas/pixel), effectiveness research (spark/rio) — activates them; until then every rubric says it's a rubric.
